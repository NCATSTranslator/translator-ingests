Can you figure out why the BindingDb ingest code doesn't seem to generate any PubChem sourced edges as KGX output?                                                                                                                                                                                               

...Here's the full diagnosis:

  ---
  Root cause: Two independent filters each eliminate all PubChem-curated records

  Filter 1 — filter_affinity_values() (bindingdb_util.py:241)
  
  PubChem contributes primarily high-throughput screening (HTS) data. HTS hits are weak binders tested at
   10–100 µM concentrations, so most IC50 values exceed the AFFINITY_FILTER_UPPER_BOUND = 1.0e+4 (10,000 nM).

  The pubchem_line.tsv sample confirms this:
  IC50 = " 15300" → after strip_chars("<> ") → 15300 nM → 15300 > 10000 → nulled.
   All other affinity columns are empty → row removed.

  Filter 2 — process_publications() (bindingdb_util.py:184–206) — sufficient on its own

  PubChem records cite their assays by PubChem AID (e.g., aid1438), which lives in column 21 of the real 640-column
   BindingDB TSV. process_publications() only checks three columns: PMID, Patent Number, and Article DOI.
    PubChem records have none of these populated. The BindingDB Entry DOI (10.7270/Q2T72FSX)
  is also not checked — only Article DOI is. With all three empty, PUBLICATION = null → row filtered at line 206.

  This second filter alone is sufficient to eliminate every PubChem-curated record, regardless of affinity values.
   The "PubChem": "infores:pubchem" entry in CURATION_DATA_SOURCE_TO_INFORES_MAPPING (bindingdb_util.py:66) is
    consequently unreachable — no PubChem record survives to the join at bindingdb.py:170.

  Secondary finding

  The unit-test TSV (tests/unit/ingests/bindingdb/BindingDB_All.tsv) is missing the real file's PubChem AID column
   (column 21 in the full data) and has no PubChem-curated rows at all, so the test suite cannot catch this regression.

  ---
  To fix, you'd need to:

  1. Add the PubChem AID column to BINDINGDB_COLUMNS and update process_publications() to generate a
   pubchem.assay:aid1438-style publication reference from it

  2. Decide whether to include weak HTS binders from PubChem (raise/remove the threshold for that data source,
   or leave filter 1 as intentional gatekeeping)
